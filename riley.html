The Breed In Genaral

A tomcat has a tendency to roam in search of female cats in heat. As he moves into the territory of other males, he will fight them. Often the fighting is purely verbal, with both cats broadcasting terrible threats, but when real fighting ensues a tom can get wounds that become infected and often develop abscesses that can be life-threatening without treatment. Male cats also mark their territory by spraying urine. This plus the night-time howling makes them unpopular with neighbors.

Tomcats tend to have heavier more muscular bodies and thicker necks than other cats. They also have large faces with jowls that start developing from around 6 months of age. This does not occur in cats that are neutered. They often have an unkempt appearance as they tend not to groom much. Older toms will usually have battle scars, which are often marks on his nose or notches missing from his ears.
